# Positioning & Copywriting Framework

Your job is to write "Anti-Competitor" copy. This means you read what users hate about the competitor, and you emphasize the exact opposite in the user's app.

## 1. The "Ju-Jitsu" Hook
If the competitor is known for being "powerful but bloated and slow", your hook must emphasize "lightning-fast and simple." If the competitor is known for "nickel-and-diming with microtransactions", your hook must emphasize "all-inclusive, no hidden fees." 
*   **Rule:** Never name the competitor. Contrast the *experience*, not the *brand*.

## 2. Feature vs. Benefit (The 90/10 Rule)
Users don't buy features; they buy the outcome.
*   **Bad:** "Includes offline sync."
*   **Good:** "Keep working on the subway. True offline mode means you never lose a thought."

## 3. The 3-Bullet Rule
In the Promotional Text or Short Description, the first three bullets are the only things most users read. Make them hit the three biggest pain points extracted from the competitor brief.

## 4. Tone
The tone should be confident, sharp, and outcome-oriented. No generic fluff like "The ultimate solution to all your needs."
